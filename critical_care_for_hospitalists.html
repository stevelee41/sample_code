Critical care for hospitalists

Sepsis/Septic shock

Defining sepsis

SIRS criteria

  • HR > 90
  • RR > 20
  • WBC< 4 or > 12.5 or > 10% bands
  • T> 38.0

IV fluids

30 ml/kg crystalloid (1.5-3 liters)

Monitoring perfusion

target MAP >= 65

clinical measures of perfusion

  • lactate
  • skin appearance
  • temperature
  • urine output
  • mental status

H's and T's - Reversible causes of cardiac arrest

H's

  • Hypoxia
  • Hypovolemia
  • Hydrogen ion (acidosis)
  • Hypokalemia/hyperkalemia
  • Hyopthermia

T's

  • Toxins
  • Tamponade
  • tension pneumothorax
  • Thrombosis (pulmonary)
  • Thrombosis (coronary)

Causes of altered mental status

  • hypoglycemia
  • infection/sepsis
  • CVA
  • seizures, consider nonconvulsive status epilepticus
  • head trauma/hematomas
    • subarachnoid hemorrhage
    • epidural hematoma
  • hypercapnia
  • severe alcohol, benzodiazepene withdrawal
  • hypertensive encephalopathy
  • medications
  • toxic ingestion
  • serotonin syndrome
  • neuroleptic malignant syndrome
